001 : Smart Street Light Using LDR || MICROPROCESSOR Project

In this project,we propose a system that will control street lights intensity based on natural light.It automatically switches ON lights when the sunlight goes below the visible region of our eyes. It automatically switches OFF lights when sunlight fall on it by using a sensor called LDR (Light Dependent Resistor) which senses the light just like our eyes.

Abstraction

Automation systems are being preferred over the manual mode because it reduces the use of energy to saves energy. These automation systems play an essential role in making our daily life more comfortable and facilitate users from ceiling fans to washing machines and in other applications. Among all exciting applications, street lights play a vital role in our environment and also plays a critical role in providing light for safety during night-time travel. In this scenario, when the street lights are in working functionality over the whole night that consumes a lot of energy and reduces the lifetime of the electrical equipment such as electric bulb etc. Especially in cities’ streetlights, it is a severe power consuming factor and also the most significant energy expenses for a city. In this regard, an intelligent lighting control system can decrease street lighting costs up to 70% and increase the durability of the equipment. We have seen that, huge amount of electricity is being wasted because of not having any control to all street lights. Basically, we are using natural environment to control over the street lights. Often we see that, the street light are remained open at broad day light and sometimes the street lights are not even switched on at night, thus many accident happens in our country. Actually, this happens because of the unconsciousness of the street light operator. So, if the system can be implemented in street lights, not only it will save huge amount of electricity but also it will make the road safer.
